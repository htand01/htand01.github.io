Highlighted Project

Chrome extension designed for users with ADHD

Inattentiveness, frequent task switching, and digital clutter pose persistent challenges for individuals with ADHD, particularly during web browsing. This project investigates how browser tab management tools can better support users with attention difficulties. Through iterative design and user feedback, I prototyped and evaluated three interventions: a tab auto-closer with color-coded visual age indicators, AI-generated tab labels, and a side panel for organizing tabs into groups. Drawing from participant input, I developed a medium-fidelity prototype that combines a visual aging system with a toggleable sidebar for tab grouping and customizable settings. This integrated design aims to help users reduce tab overload, prioritize attention, and maintain focus in multi-tab environments. Click on the thumbnail to open the project docs.
